Opinions


We are getting several comments on our editorial on the Sudanization of Nigeria earlier in the month...as well as other pertinent topics. Here are some views

 
From Lagos:
John Dimanu,
 
Let me say that because of the situation in Nigeria today, where the Hausa-Fulani oligarchy wishes to take the rest of the country down the path of Islam, it is imperative that the south of Nigeria demand a conference of the ethnic nationalities so as to once and for all determine our future. If they don't want to do that then we can't help but to split the country alomg the lines of the Sudan. By the way, Happy New Sudan! You guys deserve it...so long Sharia! The North can swallow their Sharia and bury themselves in slavery and darkness. 
 
Kano, Nigeria
Dada Adebayo
 
I have lived among Muslims in Nigeria as you can tell I am writing from one of their major cities in Nigeria.  What worries me about nothern Nigerians is how manipulative their local leaders are. They have succeeded in brainwashing their northern peoples that Christians are infidels and that southern Nigerians are bad because they are largely Christian. I am a Christian from the south and what we go through in northern Nigeria is not something that can be described in words. The only solution as far as I am concerned regarding the developmental problems in Nigeria is to split the country like Sudan so we can have peace finally.
 
Monrovia, Liberia
Mark Momodu
 
Let me first praise your effort at an excellent website for Africans and the African continent. Your story on 'Why Fela matters' is very moving. I didn't know a lot of things about this Nigerian great musician whose death pained me a lot. He was my hero and I have more albums by him than all the musicians in the world put together...and I collect a lot of music. Fela was a revolutionary who put things in perspective for the average Africa, he talked about our condition as Africans like no other personality has ever done. We need more of you (as in africanworldnewz.com) in our world to continue festering the African Personality which Fela represented so well.
