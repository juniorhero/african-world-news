Getting Your Credit Report – For Free!

By Richard Haggins, Education Manager, InCharge Canada

 

Many people are aware that somewhere out there, they have a Credit Report, which is a record of their recent usage of credit, such as using credit cards, taking out loans, or buying something and paying in instalments (e.g. car loans).  You may also be aware that your Credit Reports are checked by organizations which offer credit, such as banks, mortgage brokers, and other businesses.  Also, landlords and employers have also been known to check these Credit Reports as well.  The problem is – many people don’t know how to check their own Report.

 

You are the one who is responsible for ensuring the accuracy of your Report, so it is important that you check yours at least once a year, from both of the major credit bureaus operating in Canada.  (These credit bureaus are Equifax and TransUnion.)  Here are four methods you can use to get your Credit Report:

 

  1. By Mail – You will need to complete the Request Form, which is available on-line, and also photocopy two pieces of identification, one of which should be government-issued photo identification, such as a driver’s licence, an OHIP card, or a passport (Canadian only), or a Landed Immigrant card.  Links to the Request Forms can be found below, with the addresses for the credit bureaus.  The Credit Report will be mailed to you and will arrive within two weeks. This is a free service.
  2. By Phone – The contact phone numbers can be found in the section below.  The Report will be mailed to you, as above.  This is also a free service.
  3. In Person – If you live near the credit bureaus’ local offices, you can also go to the offices and pick up your Report in person.  The locations can be found below.  This is also free.
  4. By Internet.  You can order your Report through the credit bureaus’ websites.  This will cost approximately $15, but you will have your Report immediately.  The website addresses can be found below.
